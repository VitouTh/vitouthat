About

I am the son of a math teacher. Numbers were the most natural language in our house, and I grew up solving problems for the pleasure of it.

At 17th, I left Cambodia with scholarship offers from both home and abroad institutions. I chose abroad, and I chose Information Technology, a field I knew almost nothing about, because watching tools like Google SEO had convinced me the world had moved further than I had realised.

The world moves further.
I keep trying to catch up.

In the years that followed, I worked as a full‑stack developer, building various websites and mobile applications. This period was formative, where it taught me what engineering feels like in practice. As artificial intelligence began to rise, however, my focus shifted back to problems where mathematics, not only engineering, provides the answers. The AINS Lab gave me the environment to pursue that work.

I see academic work as a long and uncertain road, fields change in ways no one can fully predict. More than any particular method, I rely on the ability to keep learning when the ground shifts.

Research Focus
01

AI-Native Semantic Communication

Developing AI-native wireless communication systems that transmit semantic information rather than raw data. Applying Transformer-based shared embedding and attention mechanisms for semantic compression and recovery, and DRL to control resource allocation and scheduling.

02

AI‑Based Intelligent Resource Management

Designing resource control algorithms using Multi-Agent Deep Reinforcement Learning for power control, beamforming, and interference management. Learning‑based QoS assurance via Deep Learning to maintain latency, jitter, and service reliability in dynamic network environments.

03

AI for Space–Air–Ground Integrated Networks (SAGIN) & UAV Communications

Learning‑based optimization of 3D integrated networks spanning satellites, UAVs, and terrestrial systems. Joint optimization of UAV trajectories, altitudes, wireless power transfer, and beamforming using DRL with Federated Learning combined with evolutionary algorithms.
